Biography

Dr. Xiaodong Yue is a senior lecturer in the School of Computer Engineering & Science at Shanghai University. Before joining Shanghai Univ., he was a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Computer Science & Technology at Tongji University. He received the Ph.D. degree from Tongji Univ. in 2010, supervised by Prof. Duoqian Miao, and the B.Eng. from Shanxi Univ. in 2003. From Oct. 2008 to Apr. 2009, he was a research assistant in the Department of Computer Science at Hong Kong Baptist Univ., supervised by Prof. J.M. Liu. From Jan. 2011 to Oct. 2012, he was a research fellow in the Faculty of Engineering & Info Tech. (FEIT) at Univ. of Technology, Sydney (UTS), supervised by Prof. L.B. Cao.

The major research interests of Dr. Yue include the theories/applications of Pattern recognition and Artificial intelligence, in which he has the special focus on Soft Computing, Multimedia and Data mining. He has published more than 20 academic papers in these areas, including prestigious journals and international conferences. Dr. Yue has supervised 2 national research foundations of China as a principal investigator. He also joined in 5 relevant research projects and 3 engineering projects as a major participant in recent years.

Dr. Yue is a member of IEEE, CCF Artificial Intelligence & Pattern Recognition Technical Committee, CAAI Machine Learning Technical Committee and CAAI Soft Computing & Rough Sets Technical Committee. He has served as a paper reviewer/external reviewer for a number of international conferences such as ICDM, PAKDD, FUZZ-IEEE, CCPR, RSKT, FSKD, GrC, WI and served as a publication chair for ICME2012 and DSAA2014. He has also served as a reviewer for several journals such as Chinese journals “Journal of Computer Research and Development”, “Computer Science”, and the international journals “IEEE Intelligent Systems” and “Measurement”. In 2012, he has served for the National Natural Science Foundation of China as a proposal reviewer.
